Founder

The Caravelle Hotel in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam, was originally developed by a consortium of investors, including foreign and local businesspeople. It officially opened on Christmas Eve in 1959 and was initially owned by a group of French and Vietnamese investors. The hotel became well known as a gathering place for journalists during the Vietnam War. If you were referring to a different Caravelle Hotel, let me know!

- The hotel was originally owned by Saigon Tourist Company in partnership with foreign investors.

- It was designed by architects from France and Vietnam to reflect a blend of Western modernity and local elegance.

- t quickly became a hub for international journalists covering the Vietnam War (1955–1975), particularly due to its close proximity to the U.S. Embassy and major news offices.

- The hotel was known for its rooftop bar, which provided a panoramic view of Saigon and became a key social spot for diplomats and reporters.
